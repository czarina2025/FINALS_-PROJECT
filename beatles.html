The Beatles

Legendary band that changed music forever.

Band Members

The Beatles were a legendary English rock band formed in Liverpool in 1960. Comprising John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison, and Ringo Starr, they revolutionized music with their innovative songwriting, harmonies, and style, becoming one of the most influential bands in history.

John Lennon

Vocals, Rhythm Guitar

Birth: Oct 9, 1940 – Death: Dec 8, 1980

Co-founder of the band, iconic songwriter and activist. Known for hits like "Imagine" and "Strawberry Fields Forever".

Paul McCartney

Bass Guitar, Vocals

Birth: Jun 18, 1942

Known for melodic bass lines, songwriting with Lennon, and hits like "Hey Jude" and "Let It Be".

George Harrison

Lead Guitar, Vocals

Birth: Feb 25, 1943 – Death: Nov 29, 2001

Brought Indian music influences, composed hits like "Something" and "Here Comes the Sun".

Ringo Starr

Drums, Vocals

Birth: Jul 7, 1940

Known for steady drumming style, iconic fills, and charming personality.

Tours & Events

1963 — Rise to Fame

The Beatles explode in the UK with massive fandom.

1964 — First US Tour

Beatlemania hits America after their historic Ed Sullivan appearance.

1965 — Shea Stadium Concert

One of the most iconic concerts in music history with 55,000+ fans.

1966 — Final Live Tour

The band ends live concerts to focus on studio innovation.

1970 — Let It Be Premiere

The Beatles release their last album and film before split.

1964 — World Tour Begins

The Beatles tour Denmark, Netherlands, Hong Kong, Australia, and New Zealand.

1965 — UK Tour

Last proper British tour, selling out every venue within hours.

1966 — Tokyo Nippon Budokan

Historic performance at the iconic martial arts arena in Japan.

1967 — Summer of Love

Sgt. Pepper era marks a creative explosion despite no live shows.

1969 — Rooftop Concert

The Beatles perform their final live public show on the Apple Corps rooftop.
