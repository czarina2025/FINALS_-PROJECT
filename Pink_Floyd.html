Pink Floyd

Pioneers of progressive rock and psychedelic sound.

Band Members

Pink Floyd was formed in London in 1965. Their music pushed boundaries with philosophical lyrics, experimental soundscapes, and powerful concepts. The classic lineup includes David Gilmour, Roger Waters, Richard Wright, and Nick Mason.

David Gilmour

Lead Guitar, Vocals

Birth: Mar 6, 1946

Joined the band in 1967. Known for soulful guitar tones and vocal performances. Major contributions to Dark Side of the Moon, Wish You Were Here, and The Wall.

Famous Live Solo

Roger Waters

Bass Guitar, Vocals

Birth: Sep 6, 1943

Main lyricist during the conceptual era. Architect behind albums like Animals and The Wall.

Live “Comfortably Numb”

Richard Wright

Keyboards, Vocals

Birth: Jul 28, 1943 — Death: Sep 15, 2008

Crafted the atmospheric textures of the band. Composer of “Us and Them” and “The Great Gig in the Sky”.

Studio Performance

Nick Mason

Drums, Percussion

Birth: Jan 27, 1944

Only member featured on every Pink Floyd studio album. Known for experimental rhythms and technical creativity.
